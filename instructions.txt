# NGO Food Wastage Reduction Project — Detailed Description

## 1. Project Title

*Food Waste Reduction and Redistribution System for NGOs*

---

## 2. Introduction

Food wastage is one of the major global issues, while millions of people suffer from hunger every day. Restaurants, hotels, functions, and households often have surplus food that goes to waste. At the same time, many NGOs struggle to collect food efficiently and distribute it to needy people.

Our project aims to *bridge the gap between food donors and NGOs* by creating a platform where surplus food can be reported, collected, and distributed effectively.

---

## 3. Problem Statement

* Large amounts of food are wasted daily
* NGOs face difficulty in identifying surplus food sources
* No proper communication between donors and NGOs
* Lack of real-time coordination
* Food wastage leads to environmental and economic problems

---

## 4. Proposed Solution

We developed a *Food Wastage Management System* that connects:

* Restaurants
* Hotels
* Event organizers
* Individuals
* NGOs

Through this system:

* Donors can register and report surplus food
* NGOs receive notifications
* NGOs collect food
* Food is distributed to needy people

---

## 5. Objectives of the Project

* Reduce food wastage
* Help NGOs collect surplus food
* Provide food to needy people
* Create an efficient donation system
* Promote social responsibility

---

## 6. Key Features

### 1. Donor Registration

### 2. NGO Registration

### 4. Notification System

### 5. Pickup Management

### 6. Tracking System

# What Can Be Done With Expired Food

## 1. Food Segregation System (Very Important)

Your system can classify food into:

* ✅ Fresh Food → Donate to people
* ⚠️ Near Expiry → Quick distribution
* ❌ Expired Food → Alternative usage

This makes your project *more practical and safe*.

---

## 2. Composting (Best Option for Expired Food)

Expired food can be converted into:

* Organic fertilizer
* Compost for farming
* Garden manure

NGOs can collaborate with:

* Local farmers
* Community gardens
* Municipal compost units

This reduces environmental waste 🌍

---

## 3. Animal Feeding

Some expired food (not spoiled or toxic) can be used for:

* Cattle shelters
* Animal NGOs
* Stray animal feeding

But this must follow safety rules from Food Safety and Standards Authority of India.

---

## 4. Biogas Production

Expired food can be used to produce:

* Biogas
* Electricity
* Cooking fuel

Large NGOs or municipalities can use this approach.

---

## 5. Expiry Prediction Feature (Smart Idea for Your Project)

Your system can include:

* Expiry date tracking
* "Donate before expiry" alerts
* Quick pickup notifications

This reduces food becoming expired in the first place.

---

## 6. Safety Guidelines (Important for Viva)

Follow recommendations from World Health Organization:

* Do not distribute expired food to people
* Check smell and condition
* Ensure safe storage
* Maintain hygiene standards
